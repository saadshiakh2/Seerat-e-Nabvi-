Early Life Overview

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) was born in Makkah in 570 CE. His early life was marked by immense hardships, yet he grew to be known for his honesty, kindness, and wisdom.

Orphanhood and Upbringing

Born an orphan, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) lost his father before birth and his mother at the age of six. His grandfather, Abdul Muttalib, took him under his care but also passed away when he was eight. His uncle, Abu Talib, then raised him with great love and support.

Early Work and Reputation

In his youth, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) worked as a shepherd and later became a successful merchant. His honesty and integrity earned him the titles "Al-Sadiq" (The Truthful) and "Al-Ameen" (The Trustworthy). His reputation led to his marriage with Khadijah (RA).

Spiritual Reflections and Path to Prophethood

Disturbed by the moral decay of Makkah, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) would retreat to the Cave of Hira for meditation. At the age of 40, he received the first revelation.
