Prophethood in Islam

Understanding the role of Allah's chosen messengers

Who is a Prophet?

A prophet (*Nabi* in Arabic) is a person chosen by Allah to deliver His message, guide people toward righteousness, and warn against wrongdoing. Prophets receive revelations through Angel Jibreel (Gabriel) and serve as role models for their communities.

Who is Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)?

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) is the last and final prophet in Islam, sent as a mercy to all humankind. He received the Quran, the holy book of Islam, and his teachings form the foundation of Islamic faith and practice.

First Revelation

At the age of 40, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received the first revelation through Angel Jibreel (Gabriel).
